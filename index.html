During my time at CET, I was involved in development of a Calcium phosphate based biocompatible bone cement having enhanced anti-washout properties and considerable mechanical strength with sustained drug release capabilities. I spent significant time optimizing the initial powder synthesis time and conditions, thereafter, preparing the cement samples for further testing. The optimal conditions such as mixing and setting time for the preparation of cement using raw materials took multiple experiments to determine. After successful preparation of cement samples, I tested the material for itâ€™s Injectability, mechanical strength and anti-washout properties. Finally, the cement sample was tested for cell attachment and viability properties ensuring its application in vivo. Techniques employed: Fluorescence microscopy, Cell culture, PDMS chip designing, Whitlockite synthesis, Bone cement preparation, Phalloidin/ DAPI staining, Mechanical strength testing.

Synthesis and analysis of self-assembled nanopeptide hydrogels

At the Molecular informatics and design lab, IIT Guwahati. I worked on the synthesis of nano-peptides using the solid-state peptide synthesis. The precipitated nano-peptides were further conditioned for self-assembly using pH modulation, resulting in the formation of self-assembled hydrogels which were examined for peptide purity, their morphological characteristics and secondary structure formation. These hydrogels were further investigated for their function as supramolecular catalysts. Techniques employed: Solid state peptide synthesis, High performance liquid chromatography (HPLC), Fourier transform infrared spectroscopy (FTIR), Field Emission Scanning Electron Microscopy (FESEM), Matrix-assisted laser desorption/ionization-time of flight (MALDI-TOF) mass spectrometry (MS)

Antigenicity prediction of 2019- novel Coronavirus using immuno-informatics approach

I carried out this analysis using immunoinformatics tools and other computational applications to identify conserved B-cell and T-cell epitopes for COVID-19 based E- protein (Envelope protein- participates in viral assembly, release of virions and pathogenesis of the virus) which are the key targets used for vaccine development in previous infectious coronaviruses like Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome(SARS) and Middle East Respiratory syndrome (MERS). A sequence similarity analysis along with prediction of MHC I and MHC II based prediction of 9 mers that are promiscuous to various alleles of Human Luekocyte Antigens(HLAs). These peptides were further visualised in 3D structure of the protein to understand its hydrophobicity, solvation and secondary structure dynamics of the peptide.

Detection of probable carcinogens in Shawarma meat sample by GC-MS

Roasted meat acquires its distinctive roasted aroma and flavour from a process known as the Maillard reaction. Studies have shown that cooking meat at high temperature the fat and protein content of the meat can lead to the formation of carcinogenic compounds or compounds that are further metabolized in the body into carcinogens. While working on this project, I was primarily responsible for designing the extraction column using silica and glass wool for extraction of sample for GC-MS analysis and protein estimation. Techniques employed: Silica Column extraction, Lowry Protein assay, Gas chromatography-mass spectrometry

Role of oxidative stress in chemo-sensitization mediated by curcumin in colon cancer cell line.

I was involved in designing the experimental pipeline according to findings inferred previously which suggested that curcumin induces the production of radicals of reactive oxygen in colon cancer cells. Also, one of the recent publications from our lab suggested that curcumin is capable of chemosensitizing doxorubicin resistant cells. Hence, based on these ramifications our group hypothesized that Radical Oxygen species (ROS) might play an important role in curcumin induced chemosensitization in DOX resistant colon cancer cells. The attempt was made to deduce the same through our study. Techniques employed: Cell culture, Phase Contrast Microscopy, Cell Viability Assay, Rhodamine Dye Assay, DCFH-DA Assay, Western Blotting
